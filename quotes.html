Sailmaking is a dying art in todays modern world and I strive to keep tradition alive by combining the timeless skills of sailmakers with the trends and advantages of modern materials- Paul Betts

From the moment we take your order your sail details, material type and other specifics are brought right to our cutting table where we set to work transforming plans on paper into the reality of a beautiful new sail for your boat. No two sails are ever exactly alike in the handmade process of sailmaking. It is this individuality and attention to detail that separates a Leitch and McBride sail from the rest of the pack and gives you the confidence to remain in control in all weathers and situations.

A Tradition

At Leitch and McBride, we have a tradition of quality and craftsmanship in sailmaking. Although other companies can use offshore factories to assemble a sail and deliver it to your door, we compete by providing hands-on service and a high quality custom product. At Leitch and McBride, every sail is cut, assembled, and finished in North America, often at our Sidney or Vancouver lofts.
