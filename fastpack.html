Fastpack Mainsail Cover

We are certainly not the first to develop this kind of mainsail cover, but we have spent
a lot of time studying the other products on the market and repairing existing covers.
Based on years of experience, we've developed our own product.

The result is a sail cover and lazy jack system that is durable and simple to use,
providing great protection for the sail. Used in conjunction with the integrated lazy jacks
and full battens, the system provides excellent sail handling when raising and lowering
the mainsail. At the end of the day, drop the main and the lazy jacks will guide the
sail into the cover. You simply pull the heavy duty zipper along the top to close it
and you’re done.

The cover and lazy jacks are completely independent of the sail and can be removed for
repair while leaving the sail on. Conversely, the mainsail can be removed while the cover
is left on the boom making it easy to service either the main or the cover.

The cover is constructed out of Sunbrella marine canvas and has a #15 size industrial
zipper with a stainless steel slider.

Each cover is custom measured and built. Nothing is "off the shelf", so that we can
ensure the best fit possible.

 

Fastpack FAQ's

Q: How does the cover attach to the boom?

A: One of the requirements of this kind of cover is that the mainsail is loose footed. This
allows us to attach the cover to the boom using the slot in the top of the spar. Most new
mains built today are loose footed. If your existing main is not, we can easily loose
foot the sail to accommodate the cover as part of the job.

Q: Is the cover attached to the sail?

A: Our cover is completely independent of the sail. We feel that this is an important
feature. Either your main or your cover can be removed at any time for repair or
maintenance, without have to remove both items. This can be a great time saver. As well,
with this kind of design, the cover has no impact on the performance of the sail.

Q: What do I do with the cover while I am sailing?

A: Our cover can be used while sailing in two different configurations. You can unzip
the cover, raise the main, slack off the lazy jacks a bit and go sailing. Or, you can
raise the main, slack off the lazy jacks and pull them forward to the gooseneck, then
roll each side of the cover down around the internal batten and secure the roll with
the built in straps. While sailing you are left with a neatly rolled cover along each
side of the boom. Reverse the process when you are ready to lower the main.
