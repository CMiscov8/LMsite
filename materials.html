Materials
Leitch and McBride makes use of materials from Bainbridge, Challenge, Dimension Polyant and Contender. We match materials with the style of construction and the intended use of the sail.

Up Wind Sails
Dacron Cross Cut.
Provides a durable balance of performance and cost effectiveness. Built using premium woven polyester from Challenge and Dimension.
Radial Dacron.
Provides a low-stretch performance in a durable, UV and mildew resistance woven. Built using the latest in warp-oriented woven polyester from Challenge and Dimension. Radial construction results in minimized stretch and improved shape.
Radial Cruising Laminate.
Provides lower stretch, weight reduction and improved shape holding. Built using materials from Dimension. Cruising Laminates have a layer of polyester taffeta laminated on each side of a film-encapsulated scrim. The taffetas increase the durability of the sail and are coated with Dimension’s UVM to reduce mildew growth and improve UV resistance.
Race Laminate.
Provides superior shape holding characteristics, weight reduction, and advanced lamination technology. Build using a variety of Technora, Aramid and Carbon materials.

Down Wind Sails.
Cruising Asymmetric Spinnakers
Built using the latest Nylon spinnaker fabrics with panel layouts optimized for the cloth and intended use. Our cruising asymmetric is all purpose sail designed to be combined with a number of furling options or with the standard sock.
Racing Asymmetrical and Symmetrical Spinnakers.
Built using the latest Nylon, Polyester, or Code Zero materials for high tear resistance, smooth surface and stability. Our race sails are full matrix cut with optimized panel layout for maximum strength and the least amount of drag. We build a full range of race sails from light air reachers to heavy air runners.
